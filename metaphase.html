Metaphase - Mitosis Breakdown

During Metaphase, the chromosomes align along the metaphase plate (the center of the cell). This is ensures that each daughter cell will receive an identical set of chromosomes. The mitotic spindle fibers extend from the centrioles at opposite poles of the cell and attach to the kinetochores of the chromosomes, ensuring they are properly aligned before they are separated.

The chromosomes line up in the center of the cell, and the cell ensures all chromosomes are attached to the spindle fibers before proceeding to the next phase.
